A Six Minute Film

It is six minutes long.
It will stay with you a lot longer.

Winner — Best Documentary Short
Santa Monica Film Festival

SHINE asks the question almost no one is brave enough to ask out loud. What if your desires, your choices, even your thoughts were your own? What if something has been quietly shaping them all along?

The most surprising part is who is asking it. SHINE was directed by 14 year old Azai Willis. He saw something clearly that most people miss for their entire life. Then he turned it into a six minute movie so striking it won Best Documentary Short at the Santa Monica Film Festival and is now touring major US film festivals.

Azai is the son of Mikki Willis, director and producer of the Plandemic film series, one of the most watched and most censored documentary series of all time.

On June 30th, father and son sit down together, live, to talk about SHINE and the profound insights it reveals about the precarious moment we are living in, and how to recognize the traps of subliminal influence.

This is the first time anyone outside the festival circuit will see SHINE. And the film is only the beginning of the night.
